AU Discovery Call Script — Arvin Mondano · GBP & Local SEO

CLOSER framework (locked 2026-05-15) adapted for the Australia lane, 2026-07-02. Offer: STARTER US$197/mo · GROWTH US$297/mo ⭐ (intended winner) · DOMINATION from US$997/mo by-quote + Foundation Build from US$497 (see PRICING_AND_KPIS.md). Context: prospect usually replied to the cold email about THEIR map ranking, or typed "audit". This call often doubles as the 15-min audit walkthrough — the geo-grid IS your demo. Hard time-box: 20 minutes. C 2 · L 1 · O 6-8 · S 2 · E 3-4 · R 2-3. Register: Owners in these markets are direct and allergic to hype. Short sentences, concrete numbers, no corporate filler, no pressure theatrics. Never mention PH pricing, "agents", or any tool name (Local Falcon = "my rank tracker").

Pre-call (5 min before)

C — Clarify (2 min)

Q0 — always first:

"Before we dive in — my email was one of dozens you got that week. What made this one worth a reply?"

Listen for which line hooked them (the rank? the review gap? the guarantee?). That's the pain — sell to THAT for the rest of the call.

Then the pipe-analogy, AU variant:

"Quick one before the numbers — if 50 new clients found you on Google tomorrow, could you actually fit them in, or would something break first?"

Capacity answer tells you Starter vs Growth. "We'd cope easily" = under-booked = visibility pain is real. "We'd drown" = ops pain → they need the booking/win-back side too.

Forward-thinking screen (filters crisis-buyers who churn):

"And where do you want the business to be by Christmas — same size but fuller books, or growing past that?"

  • ✅ Growth framing → good client, proceed
  • ❌ "I just need to stop the bleeding this month" → crisis-buyer; be honest that reviews compound over 30-90 days, don't overpromise

L — Label (1 min)

"So just so I've got it right — when someone in {suburb} searches '{niche} near me', you're sitting around #{rank}, the top 3 are taking roughly 70% of those clicks, and that's bookings going to {competitor} that should be yours. Fair?"

Precise, not sympathetic. Let them say "yeah, exactly." Don't add problems they didn't state.

O — Overview + Live Reveal (6-8 min — deepest segment)

The Live Ranking Reveal (your killer 3 minutes)

Share your screen with THEIR geo-grid heat-map:

"This is your suburb, split into a grid — every dot is where a customer might be standing when they search '{niche}'. Green means you show top-3 on their phone. Red means you're invisible. [pause — let them count the red] You're green right here around the shop, and red from about {landmark} out. Every red dot is someone booking {competitor} instead."

Say nothing for 5 seconds. The owner does the math themselves — that silence closes harder than any pitch.

ROI probe (adapted 5-Q — capture ALL of these in the tracker as baselines)

  1. "What's an average visit worth — cut, set, treatment, roughly?" → $ ticket
  2. "How many new faces walk in per month right now?" → new clients/mo
  3. "How many of those come back regularly?" → repeat rate / LTV
  4. "Who handles your Google page and review-chasing today — you, or nobody?" → hours + neglect
  5. "Do you keep customer contact details anywhere — booking system, phone, notebook?" → list exists? (gates the guarantee — see E)

Audit questions

  1. "What have you tried already — an agency, a nephew, DIY?"
  2. "What actually moved the needle, what was a waste?"
  3. "If nothing changes, where's the Google page in 6 months?" (competitors' review counts only go up)

Label the gap, Clay verbatim adapted:

"Right — so you've got a genuinely good {niche} business, {rating}★ average proves it. You're close. You're just missing the machine that turns happy clients into reviews and reviews into map position. That's the one piece."

S — Sell the Vacation (2 min)

Outcome-first, no tool names:

"Here's what running with me looks like. Every client who walks out happy gets one tap to leave you a Google review — the count climbs every single week on autopilot. Anyone with a concern reaches YOU privately first, so you can make it right — and they can always still post publicly, nobody gets blocked. That's the Feedback Concierge. Your profile gets fully optimised so Google actually understands what you do and where. Past clients get a win-back message so quiet weeks fill themselves. And every month you get a one-page report: reviews, your map heat-map turning green, and Google's own numbers — calls, clicks, bookings."

The Amazon analogy (verbatim — works everywhere):

"Think of it like shopping on Amazon. Two products, same price — one's got 1,200 reviews at 4.9 stars, the other's got 23. Which one do you buy? Your customers do exactly that on Google Maps. We make you the no-brainer choice."

Stop talking. Let them ask the price. If they don't ask within ~30 seconds, the vacation isn't sold — go back to L and re-name the pain.

When they ask the price

Value-stack anchor first, never the number bare:

"If you bought this piecemeal from an agency — profile optimisation's about $750 a month, a review system another $600, reputation monitoring $400, the win-back campaigns and social auto-posting another $500, proper reporting $300. Call it $2,500 a month, and tools like Podium charge $450+ for just the review piece."

Then present the three levels — steer to Growth (the intended winner):

"There are three levels, and I'll tell you straight which one I'd pick for you.

Starter's US$197 a month — that's the review engine: your profile optimised, the review campaign to your past customers, the smart routing, and your monthly report. It carries the Head-Start Guarantee: 10 new 5-star reviews in 30 days or that month's free.

Growth's US$297 — that's the one most salons like yours take. Everything in Starter, plus the win-back campaigns that pull your quiet customers back in, the social auto-posting, the live dashboard, and full suburb-by-suburb rank tracking. The Head-Start Guarantee bumps to 15 reviews or free. For a hundred more, you're not just getting reviews — you're getting rebookings, which usually pays for the whole thing on its own."

(Only two guarantee conditions, ever — say them plainly: "you share your customer list so we can ask them, and you don't block the sends. That's it. Most clients land 15 to 40+.")

If they're a clinic / med-spa / dental / multi-location (Domination territory): do NOT quote a tier — quote the value.

"For a practice like yours there's a level above that — Domination. That's where we get you recommended by Google's AI when people ask for a recommendation — and picked up by ChatGPT too — plus the full SEO layer (on-page content, citations, technical) and multi-location tracking. It starts at US$997 a month and I price it off what one new patient is actually worth to you — there's a one-time Foundation Build (from US$497) to set it all up. I'll put exact numbers in your proposal after this call."

Founding scarcity (never discount — add bonuses instead):

"I'm capping the founding intake at 5 clients this month — the rate steps up every 5 after that. If you're in this month, that's your locked rate. It doesn't go up on you later when the results kick in."

E — Explain Away Concerns (3-4 min)

Rule: FIRST response is always a Meta-Model question. Never counter-pitch first.

They say You ask first Then
"Too expensive" "Compared to what?" One new regular client ≈ US$500-1,000/yr. The fee pays for itself at one to two new faces a month — the map moves more than that. If they keep pushing → run the ladder in NEGOTIATION_VALUE_MENU.md: re-anchor (agency-piecemeal ~US$1,700, guarantee = built-in risk discount) → annual 12-for-11 (terms, not price) → bonus (competitor snapshot) → LAST: drop a whole tier (Growth→Starter US$197, guarantee intact). Price never drops for the same scope; below US$197 = walk.
"I'm too busy to deal with this" "How specifically — what would it need from you?" "About 20 minutes at setup, then nothing. It runs without you — that's the whole point."
"I've been burned by SEO people before" "What did they actually do — and what did you see for it?" "Fair. That's why the Head-Start Guarantee exists: 10 new 5-star reviews in 30 days or the month's free. You'll SEE the review count and the heat-map — not a jargon report."
"Are these real reviews? Is this allowed?" — answer straight, no Meta-Model — "100% real and 100% white-hat. Every review comes from YOUR real customers — we just make asking systematic instead of hoping. Nobody gets blocked from reviewing, nothing's bought, nothing's faked. That's also why it's safe — fake reviews get businesses suspended, and I don't touch that."
"I already have a marketing agency" "What are they doing on the Google Maps side specifically?" "Keep them — ads and socials aren't what I do. I own one channel: your Google profile. Most agencies leave that half-managed, which is why the grid looks like it does."
"Why should I trust someone overseas?" "What would you need to see to be comfortable?" Timezone (city-conditional): Perth → "we're in the exact same timezone — I'm live when you're live." Sydney/Melbourne/Brisbane → "I work Australian business hours — when your shop opens at 9, I'm already at my desk." Then proof: Dream Nails ranked #1 in 9 days and, in under a month, 40+ five-star reviews; Italy + Spain clients, 5.0 across the board. "And month-to-month means I have to re-earn it every 30 days."
"Can't I just do this myself?" "Totally can — what's stopped you so far?" "Nothing I do is secret — it's consistency. Asking every single customer, every week, forever. That's what owners never sustain, and the algorithm rewards nothing else."
"I need to think about it" "No stress — what specifically would help you decide: the price, the process, or whether it'll work for {business}?" Answer THAT one thing, then Two-YES close on a follow-up slot.
"Need to talk to my partner" "When are you two next sitting down?" "I'll flick you the one-pager so you're not explaining from memory — and let's lock 10 minutes the day after. Tuesday or Wednesday?"

Don't-Solve-On-Call (when they push for the exact how mid-call):

"Good question — there are a couple of ways to run that, and I don't want to give you a generic answer. Let me test it against your actual profile and put the right one in your report. Cool?"

R — Reinforce + BAMFAM (2-3 min)

Never end without locking the next concrete action. Two-YES close every time — never "when works for you?"

Level 1 — they're lukewarm

"Easiest next step: I'll finish your full visibility report — the heat-map, your review gap against the top 3, where the ~70% opportunity is — completely free, no strings. Then 15 minutes to walk it. Thursday morning or Friday arvo?"

Level 2 — they're warm

"Let's just start. I need about 20 minutes from you: you add me as a manager on your Google profile — I'll walk you through it, it's three clicks — and you send me whatever customer contacts you've got, even if it's messy. First review requests go out within 48 hours. Want to do the setup Friday this week or Monday?"

Level 3 — onboard-first close (the strongest move)

Run the 20-min setup FIRST (GBP manager access + customer list received + first campaign queued). THEN, at the end:

"Sweet — you're live. I'll send the payment link now: [their tier — US$297/mo for Growth, US$197 for Starter], month-to-month, cancel anytime, and remember — [15 for Growth / 10 for Starter] new 5-stars in 30 days or this month's free. Once you approve it, the first requests go out tonight."

(Send the PayPal Subscriptions link for the tier they chose. The 20-min sunk-cost effect + seeing it real is what converts — same psychology as the PH onboarding-first close, no card-hostage feeling.)

Lock the calendar (AU adaptation — no self-text from a PH number):

"I'm sending the calendar invite right now while we're on — can you accept it so it's in your phone before we hang up?"

End on rapport: "What's the rest of your day look like?" → 60 seconds → "Good as gold. Talk Thursday."

After EVERY call (2 min, non-negotiable)

  1. Tracker: status → booked/won/lost + Notes: baselines (ticket $, new clients/mo, list size, who handles GBP), objections hit, next action + date
  2. Baselines are the Money Slide inputs — no baseline, no ROI proof, no case study, no V2. Non-negotiable.
  3. Won → onboarding checklist + PayPal plan + calendar the Day-14 mini-report (early proof beats waiting for Day-30)

The math you carry in your head (Money Slide, salon)

  • Top-3 map spots take ~70% of "near me" clicks (same stat as the email + audit template — never mix in other numbers)
  • Rank #1-3 + review engine ⇒ conservatively +10-15 new clients/mo
  • US$45 avg ticket, monthly repeat ⇒ US$55-80k of year-1 value ⇒ Growth is ~US$3,564/yr — about 5 cents on the dollar of the value it creates (survives an owner doing the arithmetic)
  • Clinic version: ONE implant patient ≈ US$5-50K ⇒ that's why Domination is by-quote, never flat
