The 8 rules — every message, every channel (non-negotiable)

  1. Question first. Open with a question, never a complaint or a pitch.
  2. Reveal the gap, never the fix. Name where they're losing (their Map rank, dropping off the map nearby) and stop. Never mention reviews, citations, schema, "SEO steps" — that's free consulting that kills the reply.
  3. Proof, not claims. Lead with the proof line — a named business, a real number, a tight timeframe. It's the team's result, so say "we," never "I."
  4. Stop at the question mark. No extra text after the CTA — trailing words hand back leverage.
  5. One CTA. DMs → Open to it? / Worth 5 min? (never a calendar link in a DM). Email → the booking link (only after they reply).
  6. Swap the city per prospect. Never send the wrong [[CITY]]. Match their register — plain and human, no marketing-speak.
  7. Outcome language, never tool language. "Get found first / turn 'near me' searches into bookings," never "AI / geo-grid / agent / schema."
  8. Follow up ONLY inside a live conversation. See the box below — this is the one people get wrong.

🔔 THE FOLLOW-UP RULE — read this twice.

  • ❌ They never replied to your first message → you follow up ZERO times. Leave them. Chasing people who ignored you is the fastest way to get your email / Instagram / WhatsApp / LinkedIn flagged or banned.
  • ✅ They replied, you replied back, and then they went quiet → wait 1–2 days and send ONE nudge. That's a warm chat that stalled — a single gentle nudge inside an existing thread is safe and is where most deals get recovered.
  • Cap it at one nudge. If they go dark after that, drop it. Never start a sequence.

🔄 Keep the two proof facts separate: the studio hit #1 in 9 days; the 40+ reviews built up in under a month after. Never merge them into "40+ in 9 days." (Refresh the review number as it keeps climbing.)
