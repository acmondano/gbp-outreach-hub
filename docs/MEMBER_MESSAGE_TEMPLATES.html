The 8 rules — every message, every channel (non-negotiable)

  1. Question first. Open with a question, never a pitch.
  2. Reveal the gap, never the fix. Name where they're losing (their Map rank) and stop. Never mention reviews, citations, schema, "SEO steps" — that's free consulting that kills the reply.
  3. Proof, not claims. A named business, a real number, a tight timeframe. It's the team's result — say "we," never "I."
  4. Stop at the question mark. Nothing after the CTA. Trailing words hand back leverage.
  5. One CTA. DMs → Open to it? / Worth 5 min? (never a link in a DM). Email → the booking link (only after they reply).
  6. Swap the city per prospect. Never send the wrong [[CITY]]. Talk like a human, not a marketer.
  7. Outcome language, never tool language. "Get found first / turn 'near me' into bookings," never "AI / SEO steps / schema / heat-map / agent."
  8. Follow up ONLY inside a live conversation — see the box below.

🔔 THE FOLLOW-UP RULE — read this twice.

  • ❌ Never replied to your first message → follow up ZERO times. Chasing people who ignored you gets your email / IG / WhatsApp / LinkedIn flagged or banned.
  • ✅ They replied, you replied back, then went quiet → wait 1–2 days, send ONE nudge. A warm chat that stalled is where deals get recovered.
  • One nudge, then drop it. Never a sequence.

🔄 Keep the two proof facts separate: #1 came in 9 days; the 40+ reviews built up in under a month after. Never merge them into "40+ in 9 days."

Fill-in blanks

👤 If they ask "who are you?" or "who's Arvin?"

They will — especially after clicking the link. Keep it short and confident:

"Arvin's the founder — he built the whole system and runs the results side. I'm [[YOUR NAME]], on his team, and I'll be your main point of contact start to finish. Easiest is a quick 10-min call so I can show you your map — free this week?"

Answer, reassure ("your main contact"), re-anchor to the call. Don't over-explain the setup. Never say "sales rep" — you're the specialist looking after them.

⏰ Time zones — know your client's clock

You're on Manila time. Your clients are in Australia. Send in your morning so it lands in their business hours — and always quote their local time when booking a call.
