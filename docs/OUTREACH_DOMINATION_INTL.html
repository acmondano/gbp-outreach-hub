(e) Why this works — INTERNAL RATIONALE (do not send)

  • Reveals the problem, never the fix (the core lock). Every email names what is broken — red zones on the grid, absent from the AI's answer — and stops. It never mentions content, citations, NAP, schema, or reviews. Naming the fix is free consulting that kills the reply and hands the prospect (or their current provider) the playbook. The gap creates the call; the call is where the fix lives.
  • The geo-grid heatmap is undeniable and pre-personalised. A Local Falcon screenshot of their business going red across their catchment is proof, not a claim — a 2-minute scan, not a per-prospect video. It short-circuits "we're already doing SEO," because they can see with their own eyes where they disappear.
  • Two reveals, stacked. The map shows the classic Local-Pack gap; the "I asked Google's AI and it didn't name you" line shows the newer, scarier gap. Google's AI is the measured promise, so it leads. ChatGPT is never mentioned in cold copy — it's a bonus reserved for the offer, not the hook.
  • One CTA, always the call. Book-the-call is the only ask in all four touches. No pricing, no feature list, no "reply with questions" — those leak leverage and invite an email-thread debate instead of a booked conversation.
  • Sells outcomes, not tools. "Be the name Google's AI recommends" / "stop handing customers to [Competitor]" — never "geo-grid tracking" or "schema" or any platform name. Client-facing language stays outcome-worded end to end.
  • Sequence escalates the stakes without new offers. Bump (did it land) → proof-nudge (competitor is doing something specific you're not) → breakup (early movers compound, this lead can't be bought back). The breakup's loss-frame is the highest-converting touch precisely because it still refuses to explain the fix.
  • AU-led, no US. Every example suburb/competitor is AU/NZ/UK/CA. US prospects are never cold-emailed under this brand.
